Title: China’s Reopening May Not Lead To A Major Jump In Oil Prices | OilPrice.com
Date: Tue, 17 Jan 2023 01:00:00 GMT
URL: https://oilprice.com/Energy/Crude-Oil/Chinas-Reopening-May-Not-Lead-To-A-Major-Jump-In-Oil-Prices.html
Summary:
• China's reopening unlikely to cause major jump in oil prices.
• Demand unlikely to return to pre-pandemic levels.
• Oil prices remain volatile due to a myriad of uncertainties.
• Chinese reopening and OPEC+ production cuts may provide some support.
• Transportation accounts for 54% of China's oil consumption.
• China's economic growth rate has been between 5-15% since 1992.
• China has implemented a 'zero-COVID' policy, leading to a surge in infections.
• China is looking to kick-start consumer activity and sentiment in 2023.
• China is relying on reopening and removal of negative policies, rather than aggressive stimulus.
• Demand drivers are expected to switch this year, with travel rising and property less negative.
• Net oil and refined petroleum imports are expected to increase by 5-8%.
